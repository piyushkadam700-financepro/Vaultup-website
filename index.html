Why we built this
Bank apps want to
control your spending.
We want to build your saving habit.

As a finance professional who studied in the UK, I watched friends — and myself — struggle with money not because we were bad with it, but because every tool out there tells you what not to do. Cut the coffee. Cancel the subscription. Stop going out.

Real life doesn't work like that. People don't need more guilt — they need a plan for the things they actually want. A holiday. A car. An emergency fund. A night out with people they care about.

VaultUp is built around a different assumption: that the habit of saving comes from having something worth saving for, not from being told what to cut.

What every other app does
Shows you a pie chart of your spending. Says "you spent £210 on food." Offers no plan. No context. No coaching. You already knew you spent money. That's not the problem.
What Val does differently
Knows your income, your goals, your group pots, your streak — before you ask a single question. Tells you exactly where you stand and what to do next. Specific. Actionable. Yours.

AI Money Coach — Val

Powered by Anthropic Claude. Val knows your income, spending categories, every goal's progress percentage, group pot balances, streak and XP before you ask. Real advice, real numbers, zero waffle.

Live
Goal tracking + monthly history

Set savings goals with targets and monthly commitments. Progress resets automatically each month — but your full history is permanently saved in Supabase. Val knows your ETA to every goal.

Live
Group savings with invite links

Create a group, share an invite link. Each member gets their own isolated pot — money never mixes. Group balance is the sum of individual pots. Admin controls membership. Exit = money back.

Choose your currency at sign-up or change it any time: £ GBP, $ USD, € EUR, ₹ INR, د.إ AED. Val adapts her coaching to your currency automatically — every number, every goal, every insight in your money.

Open Banking roadmap

Built for the UK's
financial future.

Architected around the FCA's Open Banking initiative (OBIE, PSD2). Each phase adds capability without a rebuild — the individual pot architecture and AI coaching layer scale through every phase.

Phase 1
Now
Live — 51 users
AI coaching + Supabase auth + group savings + multi-currency

Val coaches users based on their complete financial profile. Real user accounts with Supabase auth and Row Level Security. Group savings with individual pots and working invite links. Monthly history snapshots. Multi-currency support (£, $, €, ₹, د.إ) with full AI awareness. Zero FCA registration required — no financial activity by VaultUp.

Phase 2
6 months
Coming soon
React migration + read-only bank linking (TrueLayer)

Frontend refactor to React components. Open Banking read-only connection via TrueLayer (AISP scope — TrueLayer holds the FCA licence, VaultUp integrates via API). Val auto-detects contributions from real transaction data. No manual spending entry needed.

Phase 3
12 months
Roadmap
Automated direct debits via GoCardless

Member sets a monthly amount. GoCardless handles the Direct Debit mandate. Money moves automatically on payday. Group pot contributions happen without manual action.

Phase 4
18 months
Roadmap
Real pooled accounts via Modulr or ClearBank

Each member's group pot becomes a real ring-fenced sub-account under a partner's FCA e-money licence — own sort code and account number. Automated returns on exit via Faster Payments. And because the money is real and pooled, interest on balances becomes possible — the same structure Chip and Plum use today. VaultUp's goal is to combine that return with something they don't have: a coach who helped you save in the first place.

Phase 5
2–3 years
Roadmap
Group ISA and investment products

From saving for a holiday to building a house deposit — tax-free group investment pots for long-term shared goals. VaultUp transitions from savings app to wealth platform. The full arc from age 16 to 28, globally.

Founder
Piyush Kadam
Founder & Product Builder · Bristol, UK

VaultUp was born from a personal frustration. As someone studying finance — ACCA part-qualified, MSc in Accounting and Finance — I knew exactly how money worked in theory. Balance sheets, cash flow models, yield curves. But none of that helped me, or anyone I knew, actually save consistently in real life.

The people I was watching struggle weren't careless. They were 19, 22, 25 — in their first jobs, earning money for the first time, trying to figure it out. Nobody was talking to them. Nobody said: "You've got £1,800 coming in this month — here's where it's going and here's what you should do."

My long-term vision is simple: I want to help every individual save for what matters to them — and see VaultUp as their own vault to do exactly that. Not a bank. Not a budgeting tool. A vault. Whether they're in Bristol, Mumbai, Dubai, or anywhere else. The coaching is the same. The context is theirs.

Built solo, deployed in 8 weeks, 51 real users, zero paid acquisition — and a clear 5-phase roadmap to becoming the first AI-native wealth platform built around that belief.
